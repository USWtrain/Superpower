What is Superpower Health?
Understanding the client and their mission

Superpower is a digital preventive healthcare startup operating a "health super-app" platform focused on longevity and functional medicine. Founded in 2023, it provides affordable, data-driven, and proactive health tracking as an alternative to traditional reactive primary care. US Wellness supports Superpower by conducting the on-site blood draws that power their platform.

🧬

Biomarker Testing

Members receive annual or biannual blood draws testing 100+ biomarkers across hormones, metabolic health, inflammation, and toxins.

🔗

Data Integration

Lab results are synthesized with EHRs, genetics, and wearable data from Oura Ring, WHOOP, and other platforms.

🤖

AI Health Intelligence

Proprietary AI interprets data to calculate biological age, generate health scores, and flag early chronic disease risks.

🗺️

Actionable Roadmaps

Personalized lifestyle protocols, supplement regimens, and diet modifications tailored to each member's specific biology.

💬

Concierge Support

24/7 AI concierge and care team of licensed functional medicine clinicians accessible to all members.

Specimen Collection & Management
Step-by-step SOP — follow in exact order
⏱️
Time-Critical ReminderCentrifuge within 1 hour of draw. Deliver specimens to Quest within 4 hours of collection.
1
Perform Venipuncture

Use a straight needle as standard. Butterfly needles and discard tubes are provided for hard-stick participants.

2
Invert Tubes Gently

Invert each tube no more than ten (10) times. Further inversion may cause alterations in sample integrity.

≤ 10 inversions per tube
3
Label Every Tube by Hand

Handwrite the patient's Name and Date of Birth on each of the 6 tubes. Do not rely on printed labels alone.

4
Do Not Remove the Stopper

Tube stoppers must remain in place at all times. Never uncap tubes in the field.

5
Centrifuge SST Tubes

Centrifuge for 10–15 minutes at 1,600–2,200 RCF(g) within one (1) hour of collection.

5 SST tubes only — lavender does NOT go in centrifuge
6
Package Specimens

Place all 6 tubes from one patient into one lab bag. Insert the patient's printed lab requisition form into the front sleeve of the specimen bag.

7
Store in Cooler

Place labeled specimen bag into cooler with ice packs immediately after packaging.

8
Deliver to Labcorp

Transport all specimens to Quest within 4 hours of collection. Do not leave specimens unattended or in warm conditions.

Critical Rules at a Glance
Non-negotiable standards for every event
🧤

Gloves & Hygiene

Change gloves between every participant. Sanitize hands before gloving. Open sterile packaging in front of the participant.

🔒

Privacy (PHI)

Never say results aloud. Point to the recorded value on the results form. Blood pressure and all biometric data is PHI.

🗑️

Sharps Disposal

All lancets and any material that touched blood go directly into the sharps/biohazard container. No exceptions.

Salesforce Data Entry
Accurate, timely entry required for every participant
ℹ️
Enter All Data Same DayAll participant records must be entered in Salesforce on the day of the event. Do not batch entries or carry forward to the next day.

📝 During & After the Event

  • Pull up the participant's record before beginning any screening activity.
  • Record each completed screening as it happens — do not wait until end of day.
  • Enter the exact time each participant was seen.
  • Indicate specimen collected status (Yes/No) for each participant.
  • Flag any participants who declined, were unable to draw, or required special handling with appropriate notes in the record.
  • Document any incidents or participant concerns in the Salesforce notes field.

Hardware & Software Policy
Equipment standards and acceptable use

💻 Approved Devices

  • Use only US Wellness-issued or client-approved devices for event operations.
  • Personal phones may be used only for communication with the event coordinator — not for accessing participant data or Salesforce.
  • Tablets or laptops used for Salesforce entry must be password-protected and locked when unattended.
  • Do not leave devices unsecured in public areas at any time during the event.

🔒 Login & Access

  • Use your personal Salesforce credentials — never share login information with another team member.
  • Log out of Salesforce completely at the end of every event session.
  • Do not save passwords in shared browsers or on shared devices.
  • Report any suspected unauthorized access to your supervisor immediately.

📡 Connectivity

  • Confirm Wi-Fi or hotspot connectivity before participants arrive.
  • If internet is unavailable, record data on approved paper backup forms and enter into Salesforce as soon as connectivity is restored — same day.
  • Do not use public, unsecured Wi-Fi networks to access Salesforce or any system containing PHI.

🖨️ Printing & Requisitions

  • Lab requisition forms must be printed prior to the event. Confirm printer is operational during setup.
  • Each participant must have their own individual printed requisition — forms cannot be shared or reused.
  • Place the requisition in the front sleeve of the specimen bag before sealing.
⚠️
Equipment IssuesIf any equipment malfunctions during an event (centrifuge, printer, device), contact your supervisor immediately. Do not improvise with unapproved equipment.

HIPAA & Privacy
Protecting participant health information — always
🔴
PHI is EverythingAny information that could identify a participant and relates to their health status, lab results, or medical history is Protected Health Information (PHI). Treat all participant data as PHI.

🚫 What You Must Never Do

  • Never announce, read aloud, or verbally share any participant result — point to the form.
  • Never leave participant records, printed forms, or tubes visible to other participants.
  • Never photograph participant data, results forms, or tubes with any device.
  • Never discuss participant information with anyone who is not directly involved in that participant's care or the event operation.
  • Never transmit PHI via personal text message, email, or unsecured chat.

✅ Your Privacy Responsibilities

  • Conduct conversations about results or health information privately — position yourself so others cannot hear.
  • Ensure paper forms and result printouts are face-down when not in use.
  • Shred all unused or incorrect forms on-site using a provided shredder or secure destruction bag.
  • Log out of all systems when stepping away from a device, even briefly.
  • Report any potential privacy breach (lost forms, unauthorized access, data left unattended) to your supervisor immediately.

📋 Consent & Authorization

  • Participants must have signed Superpower's authorization and consent prior to blood draw. Verify consent is on file before proceeding.
  • If a participant has not completed consent, do not draw. Direct them to complete the authorization through the Superpower app or event coordinator.
  • Participants may withdraw consent at any time. Do not pressure or persuade.

Medical Emergency Protocol
Know the steps before you need them
🚨
Call 911 FirstIn any life-threatening emergency, call 911 immediately. Do not delay emergency services to contact a supervisor.

🩺 Common Reactions to Watch For

  • Vasovagal (Fainting / Near-Fainting): Pallor, sweating, dizziness, loss of consciousness. Lay participant flat, elevate legs, ensure airway is clear.
  • Hematoma: Swelling/bruising at draw site. Apply firm pressure with gauze, elevate the arm.
  • Allergic Reaction: Rash, hives, shortness of breath. Call 911 immediately for severe reactions (anaphylaxis).
  • Nerve Strike / Paresthesia: Sharp shooting pain or numbness during draw. Remove needle immediately, document, do not re-stick same site.
  • Excessive Bleeding: Apply continuous direct pressure. Call 911 if bleeding does not stop within 5 minutes.
1
Stop the Procedure

Immediately halt any screening activity. Secure needles and sharps in biohazard container.

2
Assess the Participant

Determine if the participant is conscious, breathing, and responsive. Keep them calm and seated or lying down as appropriate.

3
Call 911 if Needed

If the participant is unconscious, cannot breathe, has severe chest pain, or any other life-threatening condition — call 911 immediately.

4
Notify Your Supervisor

Contact your US Wellness event supervisor immediately — before or after calling 911 depending on urgency.

5
Document Everything

Record the time, participant's symptoms, actions taken, and any EMS response. Complete an incident report before leaving the event.

6
Do Not Leave the Participant Alone

Stay with the participant until EMS arrives or they are stable and have declined further assistance in writing.

📞 Emergency Contacts

  • Emergency Services: 911
  • US Wellness Supervisor: Contact provided in your event briefing packet
  • Poison Control (if applicable): 1-800-222-1222
